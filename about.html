The Story of The Otherside Ministry

Founded in 2023, The Otherside Ministry emerged from a heartfelt mission to restore hope and transform lives devastated by alcohol addiction. It was established by Pastor Millicent Allan, her husband Rev. Easter Allan, and their close friend Susan Connelly in the United States. Together, this small yet determined group, rooted in unwavering faith and a profound love for Christ, sought to address the addiction crisis gripping the Mathare Valley community in Nairobi, Kenya.

They believed that no life is too broken to be redeemed, and through God’s grace, dedicated care, and the support of an international community, transformation is possible.

The ministry’s foundation rests on core values that guide every effort: dependency on God, servanthood, teamwork, empowerment, community development, recovery, and hope.

Holistic Programs to Transform Lives

Counseling and Spiritual Guidance

Through the expertise of certified counselors and compassionate pastors, individuals receive both one-on-one and group therapy. Rev. Easter Allan plays a pivotal role in helping participants confront the root causes of their addiction, all while rediscovering their identity in Christ. The ministry’s spiritual guidance serves as a cornerstone for emotional healing and renewed purpose.

Skills Training and Empowerment

Recovery doesn’t end with healing—it thrives with empowerment. Recognizing the importance of economic independence, the ministry offers vocational training to equip individuals with valuable skills.

In early 2024, the ministry proudly launched its vocational training college, providing programs such as tailoring. By combining rehabilitation with skill-building, participants are empowered to rebuild their lives with dignity and self-sufficiency.

A Community of Faith and Transformation

The journey of The Otherside Ministry is one of love, resilience, and unshakable faith. Every step is guided by the belief that through God’s grace and the power of a supportive community, lives can be transformed.

Whether it’s offering a listening ear, practical skills, or spiritual nourishment, the ministry continues to bring light to the lives of those who need it most.
